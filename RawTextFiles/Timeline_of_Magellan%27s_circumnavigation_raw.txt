<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Timeline of the Magellan–Elcano circumnavigation</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_Magellan%27s_circumnavigation&amp;redirect=no" title="Timeline of Magellan's circumnavigation">Timeline of Magellan's circumnavigation</a>)</span></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magellan%E2%80%93Elcano_circumnavigation" title="Magellan–Elcano circumnavigation">Magellan–Elcano circumnavigation</a> was the first <a href="/wiki/Circumnavigation" title="Circumnavigation">voyage around the world</a> in human history. It was a Spanish expedition that sailed from <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> in 1519 under the command of <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a>, a Portuguese, in search of a maritime path from Spain to <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a> and across the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, and concluded by Spanish navigator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juan_Sebastian_Elcano" title="Juan Sebastian Elcano">Juan Sebastian Elcano</a> in 1522. Elcano and the 18 survivors of the expedition were the first men to circumnavigate the globe in a single expedition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn._CNN._1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn._CNN.-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hist_WasM_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-hist_WasM-2">[2]</a></sup> </p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:602px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1139" data-file-width="2246" decoding="async" height="304" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg/600px-Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg/900px-Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg/1200px-Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg.png 2x" width="600"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Magellan_Elcano_Circumnavigation-en.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><center>The Magellan–Elcano voyage. <i><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)">Victoria</a></i>, one of the original five ships, circumnavigated the globe, finishing three years after setting out.</center></div></div></div>
<p>The  Spanish fleet, the <i><a href="/wiki/Armada_de_Molucca" title="Armada de Molucca">Armada de Molucca</a></i>, that left Spain on 20 September 1519 consisted of five ships with 270 men: <i><a href="/wiki/Trinidad_(ship)" title="Trinidad (ship)">Trinidad</a></i> under Magellan, Captain General; <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=San_Antonio_(spanish_ship)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="San Antonio (spanish ship) (page does not exist)">San Antonio</a></i> under <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Cartagena" title="Juan de Cartagena">Juan de Cartagena</a>; <i><a href="/wiki/Concepci%C3%B3n_(carrack)" title="Concepción (carrack)">Concepcion</a></i> under <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gaspar_de_Quesada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gaspar de Quesada (page does not exist)">Gaspar de Quesada</a>; <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago_(spanish_ship)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Santiago (spanish ship) (page does not exist)">Santiago</a></i> under <a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Serr%C3%A3o" title="João Serrão">João Serrão</a>; and <i><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(ship)" title="Victoria (ship)">Victoria</a></i> under <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Luiz_Mendoza&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Luiz Mendoza (page does not exist)">Luiz Mendoza</a>. 
</p><p>After crossing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a> and wintering in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>  the expedition navigated the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straits_of_Magellan" title="Straits of Magellan">Straits of Magellan</a>, then crossed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific" title="Pacific">Pacific</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.
</p><p>Following Magellan's death in <a href="/wiki/Mactan" title="Mactan">Mactan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>) in 1521, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebastián Elcano">Juan Sebastián Elcano</a> took command of the ship <i>Victoria</i>, sailing back to Spain across the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, round the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a> and north along the west coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.
The circumnavigation was completed by Elcano and a crew of 18 men in <i>Victoria</i>, returning to Spain nearly three years after they left on 6 September 1522.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_the_Magellan%E2%80%93Elcano_circumnavigation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1519">1519</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_the_Magellan%E2%80%93Elcano_circumnavigation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1519">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>August 10:  Departure from <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>. 
</p><p>September 20: Departure from <a href="/wiki/Sanl%C3%BAcar_de_Barrameda" title="Sanlúcar de Barrameda">Sanlúcar de Barrameda</a>.
</p><p>December 13: Entering the bay of <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>.
</p><p>December 31: Departure from Rio de Janeiro.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1520">1520</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_the_Magellan%E2%80%93Elcano_circumnavigation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1520">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>January 10: Entering the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="Río de la Plata">Río de la Plata</a>.
</p><p>February 27: Entering Bahia de los Patos.
</p><p>March 31: Begin of the overwintering stay at <a href="/wiki/Puerto_San_Juli%C3%A1n" title="Puerto San Julián">Puerto San Julián</a>.
</p><p>April 1 and 2: <a href="/wiki/Mutiny" title="Mutiny">Mutiny</a> on <i>Victoria</i>, <i>Concepcion</i> and <i>San Antonio</i>; death of Louis de Mendoza. Later execution of de Quesada, marooning of de Cartagena.   Alvaro de Mesquita becomes captain of <i>San Antonio</i>, <a href="/wiki/Duarte_Barbosa" title="Duarte Barbosa">Duarte Barbosa</a> of  <i>Victoria</i>. 
</p><p>End of April: <i>Santiago</i> is sent on a mission to find the passage. The ship is caught in a storm and wrecked. Survivors return to Puerto San Julián. Serrano (<a href="/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Serr%C3%A3o" title="João Serrão">João Serrão</a>) becomes captain of the <i>Concepcion</i>.
</p><p>July: Encounters with the “<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patagonian_giants" title="Patagonian giants">Patagonian giants</a>” (likely the <a href="/wiki/Tehuelche_people" title="Tehuelche people">Tehuelche people</a>).
</p><p>August 24: Departure from Puerto San Julián.
</p><p>October 21: Arriving at the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Virgenes" title="Cape Virgenes">Cape of the Eleven Thousand Virgins</a>, entry to what would be known as <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Magellan" title="Strait of Magellan">Strait of Magellan</a>.
</p><p>End of October: <i>San Antonio</i>, charged to explore Magdalen Sound, fails to return to the fleet, instead sails back to Spain under <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%AAv%C3%A3o_Gomes" title="Estêvão Gomes">Estêvão Gomes</a> who imprisoned captain de Mesquita. The ship arrives in Spain on May 21, 1521.
</p><p>November 28: The fleet leaves the strait and enters the Pacific Ocean.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1521">1521</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_the_Magellan%E2%80%93Elcano_circumnavigation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1521">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>March 6: Arrival at <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a> and encounters with the <a href="/wiki/Chamorro_people" title="Chamorro people">Chamorro people</a>.
</p><p>March 16: Arrival of Magellan's expedition to one of the Philippine Islands. They headed to Suluan and dropped anchor for a few hours of respite.  <a href="/wiki/Suluan" title="Suluan">Suluan</a> is a small island in the province of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Samar" title="Eastern Samar">Eastern Samar</a>. They then next dropped anchor at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homonhon_Island" title="Homonhon Island">Homonhon</a>, another small island in the province of Eastern Samar. They were detected by the boats of <a href="/wiki/Rajah_Humabon" title="Rajah Humabon">Rajah Kolambu</a> who was visiting <a href="/wiki/Dimasaua" title="Dimasaua">Mazaua</a>, who later guided them to Cebu, on April 7.
</p><p>April 7: Arrival at the <a href="/wiki/Rajahnate_of_Cebu" title="Rajahnate of Cebu">Rajahnate of Cebu</a>.
</p><p>April 27: Death of Magellan in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mactan" title="Battle of Mactan">Battle of Mactan</a>. Serrano and Barbosa are voted co-commanders.
</p><p>May 1:  At a local banquet Barbosa and 27 sailors (including Afonso de Góis, the new captain of <i>Victoria</i> after the election of Barbosa and Serrão) are murdered and Serrao captured, later killed. The three remaining ships escape.
</p><p>May 2: There are not enough men to handle three ships, thus the worm-infested <i>Concepcion</i> is burned down. Two ships remain: <i>Victoria</i> and <i>Trinidad</i>. Gonzalo Gomez de Espinosa becomes captain of <i>Victoria</i>. Joao  Lopez Carvalho is Captain General. The ships sail to <a href="/wiki/Mindanao" title="Mindanao">Mindanao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brunei" title="Brunei">Brunei</a>.
</p><p>September 21: Carvalho is replaced by Espinosa as Captain General. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juan_Sebastian_Elcano" title="Juan Sebastian Elcano">Juan Sebastian Elcano</a> becomes captain of <i>Victoria</i>.
</p><p>November 8: Arriving at <a href="/wiki/Tidore" title="Tidore">Tidore</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moluccas" title="Moluccas">Moluccas</a>.
</p><p>December 21: <i>Victoria</i> under the command of Elcano leaves the Moluccas to return home, sailing west towards the Cape of Good Hope. <i>Trinidad</i> remains at Tidore for repairs.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1522">1522</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Timeline_of_the_Magellan%E2%80%93Elcano_circumnavigation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1522">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>January 25:  <i>Victoria</i> reaches <a href="/wiki/Timor" title="Timor">Timor</a> and starts to cross the Indian Ocean.
</p><p>April 6: <i>Trinidad</i> under the command of Espinosa leaves the Moluccas heading home sailing east. After five weeks, Espinosa decides to return to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moluccas" title="Moluccas">Moluccas</a> where he and his ship are captured by a Portuguese fleet under Antonio de Brito. However, the ship was wrecked during a storm.
</p><p>May 22: <i>Victoria</i> passes the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a> and enters the Atlantic Ocean.
</p><p>July 9: <i>Victoria</i> reaches <a href="/wiki/Santiago,_Cape_Verde" title="Santiago, Cape Verde">Santiago, Cape Verde</a>.
</p><p>September 6: <i>Victoria</i> returns to <a href="/wiki/Sanl%C3%BAcar_de_Barrameda" title="Sanlúcar de Barrameda">Sanlúcar de Barrameda</a> under the command of Elcano, two weeks shy of three years after setting sail.
</p><p>September 8: <i>Victoria</i> arrives at Seville, technically completing the circumnavigation.
</p>
