<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Astronautics</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Astronautics</b> (or <b>cosmonautics</b>) is the theory and practice of navigation beyond <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_of_Earth" title="Atmosphere of Earth">Earth's atmosphere</a>.
</p><p>The term <i>astronautics</i> (originally <i>astronautique</i> in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>) was coined in the 1920s by <a href="/wiki/J.-H._Rosny_a%C3%AEn%C3%A9" title="J.-H. Rosny aîné">J.-H. Rosny</a>, president of the <a href="/wiki/Acad%C3%A9mie_Goncourt" title="Académie Goncourt">Goncourt academy</a>, in analogy with <a href="/wiki/Aeronautics" title="Aeronautics">aeronautics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Because there is a degree of technical overlap between the two fields, the term <a href="/wiki/Aerospace" title="Aerospace">aerospace</a> is often used to describe both at once. In 1930, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Esnault-Pelterie" title="Robert Esnault-Pelterie">Robert Esnault-Pelterie</a> published the first book on the new research field.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>As with aeronautics, the restrictions of mass, temperatures, and external forces require that applications in space survive extreme conditions: high-grade <a href="/wiki/Vacuum" title="Vacuum">vacuum</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Radiation" title="Radiation">radiation</a> bombardment of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interplanetary_space" title="Interplanetary space">interplanetary space</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Van_Allen_belts" title="Van Allen belts">magnetic belts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Low_Earth_orbit" title="Low Earth orbit">low Earth orbit</a>. <a href="/wiki/Launch_vehicle" title="Launch vehicle">Space launch vehicles</a> must withstand titanic forces, while <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Satellites" title="Satellites">satellites</a> can experience huge variations in temperature in very brief periods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sellers_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sellers-3">[3]</a></sup> Extreme constraints on mass cause astronautical engineers to face the constant need to save mass in the design in order to maximize the actual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Payload_(air_and_space_craft)" title="Payload (air and space craft)">payload</a> that reaches <a href="/wiki/Orbit" title="Orbit">orbit</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Astronautics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The early history of astronautics is theoretical: the fundamental mathematics of space travel was established by <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Newton" title="Isaac Newton">Isaac Newton</a> in his 1687 treatise <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philosophiae_Naturalis_Principia_Mathematica" title="Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica">Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BMW_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BMW-4">[4]</a></sup> Other mathematicians, such as Swiss <a href="/wiki/Leonhard_Euler" title="Leonhard Euler">Leonhard Euler</a> and Franco-Italian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseph_Louis_Lagrange" title="Joseph Louis Lagrange">Joseph Louis Lagrange</a> also made essential contributions in the 18th and 19th centuries. In spite of this, astronautics did not become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Practical_discipline" title="Practical discipline">practical discipline</a> until the mid-20th century. On the other hand, the question of <a href="/wiki/Spaceflight" title="Spaceflight">spaceflight</a> puzzled the literary imaginations of such figures as <a href="/wiki/Jules_Verne" title="Jules Verne">Jules Verne</a> and <a href="/wiki/H._G._Wells" title="H. G. Wells">H. G. Wells</a>. At the beginning of the 20th century, <a href="/wiki/Russian_cosmism" title="Russian cosmism">Russian cosmist</a> <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Tsiolkovsky" title="Konstantin Tsiolkovsky">Konstantin Tsiolkovsky</a> derived the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rocket_equation" title="Rocket equation">rocket equation</a>, the governing <a href="/wiki/Equation" title="Equation">equation</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Rocket" title="Rocket">rocket-based propulsion</a>, enabling computation of the final velocity of a rocket from the mass of spacecraft (<span class="mwe-math-element"><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;"><math alttext="{\displaystyle m_{1}}" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">

<p>By the early 1920s, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Goddard" title="Robert Goddard">Robert Goddard</a> was developing <a href="/wiki/Liquid-propellant_rocket" title="Liquid-propellant rocket">liquid-propellant rockets</a>, which would in a few brief decades become a critical component in the designs of such famous rockets as the <a href="/wiki/V-2_rocket" title="V-2 rocket">V-2</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saturn_V" title="Saturn V">Saturn V</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Robert_Esnault-Pelterie#Prix_REP-Hirsch" title="Robert Esnault-Pelterie">Prix d'Astronautique</a> (Astronautics Prize) awarded by the <a href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_astronomique_de_France" title="Société astronomique de France">Société astronomique de France</a>, the French astronomical society, was the first prize on this subject. The international award, established by aviation and astronautical pioneer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Esnault-Pelterie" title="Robert Esnault-Pelterie">Robert Esnault-Pelterie</a> and André-Louis Hirsch, was given from 1929-1939 in recognition of the study of interplanetary travel and astronautics.
</p><p>By the mid 1950s, the <a href="/wiki/Space_Race" title="Space Race">Space Race</a> between the USSR and the USA had begun.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subdisciplines">Subdisciplines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Astronautics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subdisciplines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although many regard astronautics itself as a rather specialized subject, engineers and scientists working in this area must be knowledgeable in many distinct fields.
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astrodynamics" title="Astrodynamics">Astrodynamics</a>: the study of orbital motion. Those specializing in this field examine topics such as spacecraft trajectories, ballistics and celestial mechanics.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spacecraft_propulsion" title="Spacecraft propulsion">Spacecraft propulsion</a>: how spacecraft change orbits, and how they are launched. Most spacecraft have some variety of <a href="/wiki/Rocket_engine" title="Rocket engine">rocket engine</a>, and thus most research efforts focus on some variety of <a href="/wiki/Rocket" title="Rocket">rocket propulsion</a>, such as chemical, nuclear or electric.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spacecraft_design" title="Spacecraft design">Spacecraft design</a>: a specialized form of <a href="/wiki/Systems_engineering" title="Systems engineering">systems engineering</a> that centers on combining all the necessary subsystems for a particular <a href="/wiki/Launch_vehicle" title="Launch vehicle">launch vehicle</a> or <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellite</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Control_engineering" title="Control engineering">Controls</a>: keeping a satellite or rocket in its desired orbit (as in spacecraft navigation) and orientation (as in <a href="/wiki/Attitude_control" title="Attitude control">attitude control</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Space_environment" title="Space environment">Space environment</a>: although more a sub-discipline of <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a> rather than astronautics, the effects of <a href="/wiki/Space_weather" title="Space weather">space weather</a> and other environmental issues constitute an increasingly important field of study for spacecraft designers.</li></ul>
