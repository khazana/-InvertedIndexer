<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Religion in space</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">Astronauts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">cosmonauts</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spaceflight_participant" title="Spaceflight participant">spaceflight participants</a> have observed their religions while in space; sometimes publicly, sometimes privately. Religious adherence in outer space poses unique challenges and opportunities for practitioners. Space travelers have reported profound changes in the way they view their faith related to the <a href="/wiki/Overview_effect" title="Overview effect">overview effect</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> while some secular groups have criticized the use of government spacecraft for religious activities by astronauts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Apollo_8_Genesis_Reading">Apollo 8 Genesis Reading</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_space&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Apollo 8 Genesis Reading">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><div class="mediaContainer" style="width:220px"><audio class="kskin" controls="" data-durationhint="115.64444444444" data-mwprovider="wikimediacommons" data-mwtitle="Apollo_8_genesis_reading.ogg" data-startoffset="0" id="mwe_player_0" preload="none" style="width:220px"><source data-bandwidth="36617" data-height="0" data-shorttitle="Ogg source" data-title="Original Ogg file (37 kbps)" data-width="0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2e/Apollo_8_genesis_reading.ogg" type='audio/ogg; codecs="vorbis"'/><source data-bandwidth="81800" data-height="0" data-shorttitle="MP3" data-title="MP3" data-transcodekey="mp3" data-width="0" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/2/2e/Apollo_8_genesis_reading.ogg/Apollo_8_genesis_reading.ogg.mp3" type="audio/mpeg"/><track data-dir="ltr" data-mwprovider="wikimediacommons" data-mwtitle="TimedText:Apollo_8_genesis_reading.ogg.en.srt" kind="subtitles" label="English (en) subtitles" src="https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?title=TimedText:Apollo_8_genesis_reading.ogg.en.srt&amp;action=raw&amp;ctype=text%2Fx-srt" srclang="en" type="text/x-srt"/></audio></div> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Apollo_8_genesis_reading.ogg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Apollo 8 Genesis reading.</div></div></div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apollo_8_Genesis_Reading" title="Apollo 8 Genesis Reading">Apollo 8 Genesis Reading</a></div>
<p>On Christmas Eve, 1968 astronauts <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bill_Anders" title="Bill Anders">Bill Anders</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Lovell" title="Jim Lovell">Jim Lovell</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Borman" title="Frank Borman">Frank Borman</a> read from the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Genesis" title="Book of Genesis">Book of Genesis</a> as <a href="/wiki/Apollo_8" title="Apollo 8">Apollo 8</a> orbited the Moon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A lawsuit by <a href="/wiki/American_Atheists" title="American Atheists">American Atheists</a> founder <a href="/wiki/Madalyn_Murray_O%27Hair" title="Madalyn Murray O'Hair">Madalyn Murray O'Hair</a> alleged that the observance amounted to a government endorsement of religion in violation of the <a href="/wiki/First_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="First Amendment to the United States Constitution">First Amendment</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aldrin_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-aldrin-4">[4]</a></sup> but the case was dismissed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Christianity">Christianity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_space&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Christianity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Christmas_on_the_International_Space_Station" title="Christmas on the International Space Station">Christmas on the International Space Station</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ISS_christmas_2009.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2848" data-file-width="4288" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/ISS_christmas_2009.jpg/220px-ISS_christmas_2009.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/ISS_christmas_2009.jpg/330px-ISS_christmas_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/ISS_christmas_2009.jpg/440px-ISS_christmas_2009.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ISS_christmas_2009.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>ISS crew with festive Christmas hats aboard <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">Zvezda</a> service module of ISS in 2009.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ISS-26_Christmas_morning.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2848" data-file-width="4288" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/ISS-26_Christmas_morning.jpg/220px-ISS-26_Christmas_morning.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/ISS-26_Christmas_morning.jpg/330px-ISS-26_Christmas_morning.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/ISS-26_Christmas_morning.jpg/440px-ISS-26_Christmas_morning.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ISS-26_Christmas_morning.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Christmas morning in Node 3 in 2010.</div></div></div>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/STS-128" title="STS-128">STS-128</a> astronaut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patrick_Forrester" title="Patrick Forrester">Patrick Forrester</a> brought a fragment of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Missionary_Aviation_Fellowship" title="Missionary Aviation Fellowship">Missionary Aviation Fellowship</a> aircraft which crashed in Ecuador in 1956.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Protestantism">Protestantism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_space&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Protestantism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Apollo_11" title="Apollo 11">Apollo 11</a> astronaut <a href="/wiki/Buzz_Aldrin" title="Buzz Aldrin">Buzz Aldrin</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presbyterian" title="Presbyterian">Presbyterian</a>, performed a <a href="/wiki/Eucharist" title="Eucharist">communion</a> service for himself using a kit provided by his church. Aldrin had told flight director <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Kraft" title="Chris Kraft">Chris Kraft</a> of his plans and intended to broadcast the service back to Earth but opted not to at the request of <a href="/wiki/Deke_Slayton" title="Deke Slayton">Deke Slayton</a>, due to the continuing controversy over Apollo 8's reading.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>A microfilm <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a> that had been to the surface of the Moon was auctioned off in 2011
.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nicola_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-nicola-7">[7]</a></sup> It was a <a href="/wiki/King_James_Version" title="King James Version">King James Version</a> created after three astronauts lost their lives in the <a href="/wiki/Apollo_1" title="Apollo 1">Apollo 1</a> fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nicola_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-nicola-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ed_White_(astronaut)" title="Ed White (astronaut)">Ed White</a>, one of the astronauts who perished, had wanted to take a Bible to the Moon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nicola_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-nicola-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_space&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Roman Catholicism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A signed message from <a href="/wiki/Pope_Paul_VI" title="Pope Paul VI">Pope Paul VI</a> was included among statements from dozens of other world leaders left on the moon on a silicon disk during the <a href="/wiki/Apollo_11" title="Apollo 11">Apollo 11</a> mission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Following the mission <a href="/wiki/William_Donald_Borders" title="William Donald Borders">William Donald Borders</a>, Bishop of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Orlando" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Orlando">Roman Catholic Diocese of Orlando</a>, told the Pope that the <a href="/wiki/1917_Code_of_Canon_Law" title="1917 Code of Canon Law">1917 Code of Canon Law</a> placed the moon within his diocese, as the first explorers had departed from Cape Kennedy which was under his jurisdiction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2011, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">Pope Benedict XVI</a> of the Catholic Church talked to the crew of the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">Space Shuttle <i>Endeavour</i></a> while it was in Earth orbit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thom_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-thom-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Russian_Orthodoxy">Russian Orthodoxy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_space&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Russian Orthodoxy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Russian Orthodox Christmas was celebrated on the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>, on January 7, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russianchristmas_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-russianchristmas-11">[11]</a></sup>  Cosmonauts had the day off, but one of the other crew <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweeted</a>, "Merry Christmas to all Russia."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russianchristmas_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-russianchristmas-11">[11]</a></sup> The whole crew also celebrated on December 25, two weeks prior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russianchristmas_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-russianchristmas-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Islam">Islam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_space&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Islam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Muslims in space struggle with fulfilling their religious obligations including kneeling and facing Mecca to pray in microgravity traveling at several kilometres per second. The issue first came up when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultan_bin_Salman_bin_Abdulaziz_Al_Saud" title="Sultan bin Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud">Sultan bin Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud</a>, a <a href="/wiki/House_of_Saud" title="House of Saud">Saudi</a> prince, flew aboard <a href="/wiki/STS-51-G" title="STS-51-G">STS-51-G</a> and again when <a href="/wiki/Anousheh_Ansari" title="Anousheh Ansari">Anousheh Ansari</a> flew as a tourist to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_space_station" title="International space station">International space station</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In preparation for Malaysian  <a href="/wiki/Sheikh_Muszaphar_Shukor" title="Sheikh Muszaphar Shukor">Sheikh Muszaphar Shukor</a>'s trip to the ISS in 2007, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Fatwa_Council" title="National Fatwa Council">National Fatwa Council</a> created  "Muslim Obligations in the International Space Station" outlining permissible modifications to rituals such as kneeling when praying (not required in space), facing Mecca when praying (left to the astronaut's best abilities at the start of prayer), and washing (a wet towel will suffice).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2014, the General Authority of Islamic Affairs and Endowment (GAIAE)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="no country specified (August 2017)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> issued a <a href="/wiki/Fatwa" title="Fatwa">fatwa</a> forbidding devout Muslims from participating as crew members in <a href="/wiki/Mars_One" title="Mars One">Mars One</a>'s proposed one way mission to <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a>. Speaking for the clerical group, Farooq Hamada explained that, "Protecting life against all possible dangers and keeping it safe is an issue agreed upon by all religions and is clearly stipulated in verse 4/29 of the Holy Quran: Do not kill yourselves or one another. Indeed, Allah is to you ever Merciful."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
