<p>The <i><b>French space program</b></i> includes both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commercial_spaceflight" title="Commercial spaceflight">civil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> <a href="/wiki/Spaceflight" title="Spaceflight">spaceflight</a> activities. It is the 3rd oldest institutional space program in history, along with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US" title="US">US</a>; and the largest space program in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=French_space_program&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fus%C3%A9e_sur_tapisserie.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="513" data-file-width="635" decoding="async" height="178" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Fus%C3%A9e_sur_tapisserie.png/220px-Fus%C3%A9e_sur_tapisserie.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Fus%C3%A9e_sur_tapisserie.png/330px-Fus%C3%A9e_sur_tapisserie.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Fus%C3%A9e_sur_tapisserie.png/440px-Fus%C3%A9e_sur_tapisserie.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fus%C3%A9e_sur_tapisserie.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Space rocket imagined on a <a href="/wiki/Gobelins_Manufactory" title="Gobelins Manufactory">Gobelins</a> <a href="/wiki/Tapestry" title="Tapestry">tapestry</a>, ordered by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Colbert" title="Jean-Baptiste Colbert">Colbert</a> and drawn by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Le_Brun" title="Charles Le Brun">Le Brun</a>, 1664.</div></div></div>
<p>Space travel has long been a significant ambition in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_culture" title="French culture">French culture</a>. From the <a href="/wiki/Gobelins_Manufactory" title="Gobelins Manufactory">Gobelins</a>' 1664 tapestry representing a space rocket,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/Jules_Verne" title="Jules Verne">Jules Verne</a>'s 1865 novel <i><a href="/wiki/From_the_Earth_to_the_Moon" title="From the Earth to the Moon">De la terre à la lune</a></i> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_M%C3%A9li%C3%A8s" title="George Méliès">George Méliès</a>' 1902 movie <i><a href="/wiki/A_Trip_to_the_Moon" title="A Trip to the Moon">Voyage dans la lune</a></i>, space and <a href="/wiki/Rocket" title="Rocket">rocketry</a> were present in French society long before the technological means appeared to allow the development of a space exploration program.
</p><p>During the late 18th century, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Fran%C3%A7ois_Pil%C3%A2tre_de_Rozier" title="Jean-François Pilâtre de Rozier">Jean-François Pilâtre de Rozier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Charles" title="Jacques Charles">Jacques Charles</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Montgolfier_brothers" title="Montgolfier brothers">Montgolfier brothers</a> are seen as worldwide precursors and explorers of <a href="/wiki/Aeronautics" title="Aeronautics">aeronautics</a>, with the world record altitude then reached by a human at 7,016 metres (23,018 ft) performed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseph-Louis_Gay-Lussac" title="Joseph-Louis Gay-Lussac">Joseph-Louis Gay-Lussac</a> in 1804. Those names, their numerous students and their works will mark the early expertise of France's space program in all types of air balloons since.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:EsnaultPelterieTimbre.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="255" data-file-width="448" decoding="async" height="125" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/EsnaultPelterieTimbre.png/220px-EsnaultPelterieTimbre.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/EsnaultPelterieTimbre.png/330px-EsnaultPelterieTimbre.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/EsnaultPelterieTimbre.png/440px-EsnaultPelterieTimbre.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:EsnaultPelterieTimbre.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/La_Poste_(France)" title="La Poste (France)">French postal</a> stamp honouring <a href="/wiki/Robert_Esnault-Pelterie" title="Robert Esnault-Pelterie">Robert Esnault-Pelterie</a>, 1967.</div></div></div>
<p>In the beginning of the twentieth century, the origins of the French space program are tied to French technological developments in <a href="/wiki/Aerospace" title="Aerospace">aerospace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Astronautics" title="Astronautics">astronautics</a>, notably the nascent <a href="/wiki/Airplane" title="Airplane">airplane</a> and rocket industries.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:EA-EOLE_rockets.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="399" data-file-width="270" decoding="async" height="325" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/EA-EOLE_rockets.png/220px-EA-EOLE_rockets.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/15/EA-EOLE_rockets.png 1.5x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:EA-EOLE_rockets.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>EA-EOLE rockets (as tested in 1940–1941 and 1951)</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Robert_Esnault-Pelterie" title="Robert Esnault-Pelterie">Robert Esnault-Pelterie</a> appears as one of the early pioneers in space exploration design and rocket science. From 1908, he studied propulsion and space flight; without knowing the work of Russian mathematician <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Tsiolkovsky" title="Konstantin Tsiolkovsky">Konstantin Tsiolkovsky</a> at that time, he derived the mathematical equations for interplanetary flight, flight durations, and engine propulsion, and was later nominated President of the <i>Chambre Syndicale des Industries Aéronautiques</i> (Trade association of Aircraft industries) in 1912.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> From 1935 to 1939 he designed a high-altitude <a href="/wiki/Sounding_rocket" title="Sounding rocket">sounding rocket</a>, but <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> interrupted his plans; German experts believed that the rocket could have reached its design goal of 60 miles (97 km).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ley196406_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ley196406-3">[3]</a></sup> Esnault-Pelterie convinced physicist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jean-Jacques_Barr%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jean-Jacques Barré (page does not exist)">Jean-Jacques Barré</a>, a pioneer in rocket propulsion, to collaborate on the design of a self-propelled cryogenic rocket.
Between 1927 and 1933, Barré did extensive research and developed a rocket that could reach the upper atmosphere and space, the EA-41 <i>Eole</i> (see picture).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Villain,_1993_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Villain,_1993-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=French_space_program&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The beginning of the institutional French space program dates back to 1946 when, right after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Laboratoire_de_recherches_balistiques_et_a%C3%A9rodynamiques&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Laboratoire de recherches balistiques et aérodynamiques (page does not exist)">Laboratoire de recherches balistiques et aérodynamiques</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laboratoire_de_recherches_balistiques_et_a%C3%A9rodynamiques" title="fr:Laboratoire de recherches balistiques et aérodynamiques">fr</a>]</span> (LRBA) was created in <a href="/wiki/Vernon,_Eure" title="Vernon, Eure">Vernon</a> to develop the next generation of rockets, partly taking advantage of the German development of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/V2_rocket" title="V2 rocket">V2 rocket</a>.
</p><p>Before this and during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWII" title="WWII">war</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Free_France" title="Free France">Free France</a> continued to function, the EA-41 was tested and improved by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_French_Forces" title="Free French Forces">military personnel</a>, from October 1942 through to 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chronologie_Ariane_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chronologie_Ariane-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>22 May 1952: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veronique_(rocket)" title="Veronique (rocket)">Véronique N1</a> is successfully launched from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sahara_desert" title="Sahara desert">Saharan desert</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chronologie_Ariane_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Chronologie_Ariane-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1958, <a href="/wiki/President_of_France" title="President of France">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Gaulle" title="Charles de Gaulle">Charles de Gaulle</a> directed the creation of several space research committees. In 1959, the Comité d'études spatiales was born under the supervision of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pierre_Auger" title="Pierre Auger">Pierre Auger</a>.
In 1961, de Gaulle signed the creation of the Centre National d' Etudes Spatiales (<a href="/wiki/CNES" title="CNES">CNES</a>) to coordinate French space activities. Development of Western Europe's first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carrier_rocket" title="Carrier rocket">carrier rocket</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Diamant" title="Diamant">Diamant</a>, began in 1962, first launched in Algeria.
</p><p>November 26, 1965: at 15h 47mn and 21s, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asterix_(satellite)" title="Asterix (satellite)">Asterix</a>, the first French satellite in space, is successfully launched by a Diamant rocket from the Algerian desert. It is active for 2 consecutive days before ceasing to transmit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chronologie_Ariane_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Chronologie_Ariane-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1965, France's space <a href="/wiki/Launch_pad" title="Launch pad">launch pads</a> and CNES settled in <a href="/wiki/Kourou" title="Kourou">Kourou</a>.
</p><p>In 1973, France impulsed the creation of the <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">European Space Agency</a> and became its first contributor.
</p><p>The French space <a href="/wiki/Budget" title="Budget">budget</a>, although stagnant since the early 2000s in constant <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">euros</a>, remains in absolute terms the largest of the member countries of the European Space Agency (ESA) and the second largest national budget (after the United States of America) at 2,33 Billion €.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2004, this budget stood at 1.698 billion euros, with 685 million being transferred to the Paris-based ESA for the programs conducted under its supervision.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ariane_rocket_at_Bourget_airport_museum,_Paris.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1936" data-file-width="2592" decoding="async" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Ariane_rocket_at_Bourget_airport_museum%2C_Paris.JPG/220px-Ariane_rocket_at_Bourget_airport_museum%2C_Paris.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Ariane_rocket_at_Bourget_airport_museum%2C_Paris.JPG/330px-Ariane_rocket_at_Bourget_airport_museum%2C_Paris.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Ariane_rocket_at_Bourget_airport_museum%2C_Paris.JPG/440px-Ariane_rocket_at_Bourget_airport_museum%2C_Paris.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ariane_rocket_at_Bourget_airport_museum,_Paris.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ariane rocket at <a href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9e_de_l%E2%80%99air_et_de_l%E2%80%99espace" title="Musée de l’air et de l’espace">Bourget airport museum</a>, Paris</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ariane_(rocket_family)" title="Ariane (rocket family)">Ariane</a> rocket family is France's own launcher family, which use has been extended to the whole of ESA's member countries.
</p><p>Its spaceport, near <a href="/wiki/Kourou" title="Kourou">Kourou</a>, was selected in 1964 to host all of France's launches. Later, it was selected as ESA's launch site. Before being in <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>, France's space launches were made from <a href="/wiki/French_Algeria" title="French Algeria">Algeria</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colomb-B%C3%A9char" title="Colomb-Béchar">Colomb-Béchar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hammaguir" title="Hammaguir">Hammaguir</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The French space program thus benefits from the best ground position for launch sites on Earth, as its position 5.3° north of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a> allows rockets to gain propulsion from the spinning of the Earth when launched eastward (+460 m/s) and save on propellant. No other governmental launch sites allow this level of physical parameters. It is also able to launch satellites into <a href="/wiki/Polar_orbit" title="Polar orbit">polar orbits</a> from this spaceport.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaborations">Collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=French_space_program&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Columbus_module_-_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2546" data-file-width="3828" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Columbus_module_-_cropped.jpg/220px-Columbus_module_-_cropped.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Columbus_module_-_cropped.jpg/330px-Columbus_module_-_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Columbus_module_-_cropped.jpg/440px-Columbus_module_-_cropped.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Columbus_module_-_cropped.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Columbus module – cropped</div></div></div>
<p>France's public involvement in space technologies is also deep into European programs such as <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Man-Tended_Free_Flyer" title="Columbus Man-Tended Free Flyer">Columbus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">Automated Transfer Vehicle</a>, through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thales_Alenia" title="Thales Alenia">Thales Alenia</a>, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Cannes" title="Cannes">Cannes</a>.
</p><p>The French space program includes collaborations between its institutions and other countries, European as well as other foreign countries and institutions (<a href="/wiki/JAXA" title="JAXA">JAXA</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISRO" title="ISRO">ISRO</a>, <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>, <a href="/wiki/China_National_Space_Administration" title="China National Space Administration">CNSA</a>) in projects ranging from the <a href="/wiki/Herschel_Space_Observatory" title="Herschel Space Observatory">Herschel Space Observatory</a> to <a href="/wiki/BepiColombo" title="BepiColombo">BepiColombo</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Saral/Altika&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Saral/Altika (page does not exist)">Saral/Altika</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Planck_(spacecraft)" title="Planck (spacecraft)">Planck space observatory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/COP21" title="COP21">COP21</a>, CNES and ISRO impulsed a groundbreaking and worldwide plan to unite all space agencies for the gathering of satellite information and detection on <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions, allowing more precise measurements and decision making.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition, CNES and ESA have a strong background of collaboration, notably building the largest single satellite surveyance program for earth's biological monitoring (<a href="/wiki/Copernicus_Programme" title="Copernicus Programme">Copernicus Programme</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_projects_and_international_collaborations">Future projects and international collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=French_space_program&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Future projects and international collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The construction of the Ariane 6 launcher is underway, after being approved by all ESA countries in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>  It is designed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Airbus_Safran_Launchers" title="Airbus Safran Launchers">Airbus Safran Launchers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2010, France and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> have been collaborating on several space missions, including long-run science programmes like <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cardiomed&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cardiomed (page does not exist)">Cardiomed</a>, dedicated to monitoring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardiovascular" title="Cardiovascular">cardiovascular</a> health in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmonauts" title="Cosmonauts">cosmonauts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>CNES has provided essential instruments (cameras) on an Indian mission to the moon, launched in January 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
A consortium led by the CNES also built <a href="/wiki/Argos_system" title="Argos system">Argos</a> instruments on board the Indian mission <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oceansat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oceansat (page does not exist)">Oceansat</a> 3 in 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> 
A third collaboration between the ISRO and French space actors (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Laboratoire_d%E2%80%99%C3%A9tudes_spatiales_et_d%E2%80%99instrumentation_en_astrophysique&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Laboratoire d’études spatiales et d’instrumentation en astrophysique (page does not exist)">Laboratoire d’études spatiales et d’instrumentation en astrophysique</a> (Lesia), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CNRS" title="CNRS">CNRS</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Universit%C3%A9_Paris-VI&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Université Paris-VI (page does not exist)">Université Paris-VI</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Universit%C3%A9_Paris-VII&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Université Paris-VII (page does not exist)">Université Paris-VII</a>) has seen the launch of <a href="/wiki/PicSat" title="PicSat">PicSat</a> in January 2018, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cubesat" title="Cubesat">nano-satellite</a> that surveys the <a href="/wiki/Beta_Pictoris" title="Beta Pictoris">Beta Pictoris</a> star for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exoplanets" title="Exoplanets">exoplanets</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
<table class="box-Update plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Update" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="Ambox current red.svg" data-file-height="290" data-file-width="360" decoding="async" height="34" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/42px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/63px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/84px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 2x" width="42"/></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section needs to be <b>updated</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please update this article to reflect recent events or newly available information.</span> <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">January 2019</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>In 2018, CNES and JAXA will launch the BepiColombo mission to study the magnetic field of <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(planet)" title="Mercury (planet)">Mercury</a> and map its surface.
</p><p>In 2018, <a href="/wiki/Solar_Orbiter" title="Solar Orbiter">Solar Orbiter</a> will be launched by NASA, containing instruments designed by CNES and other French industrial actors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Starting in 2018, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CFOSAT&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CFOSAT (page does not exist)">CFOSAT</a> (China-France Oceanography SATellite) will be placed into Earth orbit to study <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">ocean</a> surface winds and <a href="/wiki/Wind_wave" title="Wind wave">waves</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
After <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Macron" title="Emmanuel Macron">President Macron</a>'s state visit to China in January 2018, the French-Chinese collaboration in space was increased significantly and includes more in-depth collaboration, notably in the sharing of CFOSAT data, meant to study oceans and their interaction with the atmosphere, as well as in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SVOM" title="SVOM">SVOM</a> program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>The French mission Taranis (with international laboratories collaboration) will be also launched in 2018. It is the 1st satellite designed to observe lightning at altitudes of 20 to 100 km.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>The French space agency was also responsible for the construction of the main instruments on the French-German-American <a href="/wiki/InSight" title="InSight">InSight</a> mission to Mars, scheduled for launch on the 5th May 2018 and due for arrival in November 2018.
</p><p>In 2021 France and <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> will launch their collaborative mission <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Merlin_(spacecraft)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Merlin (spacecraft) (page does not exist)">Merlin</a>, due to study <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a> levels and concentrations in the earth's <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">atmosphere</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p><p>2022 will mark the launch of <a href="/wiki/Jupiter_Icy_Moons_Explorer" title="Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer">JUICE</a> (JUpiter ICy moons Explorer). It will study <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a> and three of its moons with a view to gaining new insights into how life emerged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> This mission is a collaboration of CNES with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CNRS" title="CNRS">CNRS</a> and ESA.
</p>
